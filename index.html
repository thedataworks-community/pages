Public data is messy, scattered, and hard to use. As a result, nonprofits, researchers, and businesses end up wasting valuable time wrangling and cleaning data instead of using it.

DataWorks makes public data accessible, structured, and ready to use. Our DataWorks Commons platform solves the access problem, and our custom solutions help organizations turn raw data into real impact.

The DataWorks Commons

We’ve built DataWorks Commons, a unified, intelligent platform that aggregates and standardizes fragmented state and local datasets across key areas like education, courts, social services, health, economy, and community safety. This dramatically reduces the time and cost of data acquisition for businesses, nonprofits, researchers, and journalists, allowing them to focus on their mission and drive impact.

Oklahoma once had the highest rate of female incarceration in the United States. Today, its two largest counties tell a story of change. Oklahoma County has seen a 76% drop in female prison sentences since the mid-2010s, reaching record lows in 2024. Tulsa County has cut sentences nearly 50% since 2010, but recent trends suggest progress has slowed.

As Oklahoma County continues to decline and Tulsa’s numbers level off, these trends reflect the shifting landscape of justice reform and its role in breaking cycles of incarceration.
